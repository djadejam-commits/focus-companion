## ðŸŽ¯ PART 3: Recording Best Practices

### Pre-Recording Checklist

**Environment:**
- [ ] Quiet room (no background noise)
- [ ] Good lighting (natural light or desk lamp)
- [ ] Door closed, phone on silent (your other phone!)
- [ ] Pet/family members know you're recording

**Tech:**
- [ ] iPhone connected via cable to Mac
- [ ] iPhone unlocked and on Dashboard
- [ ] QuickTime showing iPhone screen clearly
- [ ] Microphone levels tested (70-80% peak)
- [ ] This script open on Mac for reference

**Personal:**
- [ ] Hydrated (water nearby, no ice cubes!)
- [ ] Comfortable position
- [ ] Relaxed (not rushed)
- [ ] Smiled (it helps your voice sound warm!)

### Recording Strategy

**Option A: Single Take (Advanced)**
- Record entire 3-minute script in one go
- Requires practice (3-5 run-throughs first)
- Pros: Natural flow, authentic
- Cons: One mistake = full re-record

**Option B: Scene-by-Scene (RECOMMENDED)**
- Record each scene separately
- QuickTime: Stop/Start between scenes
- Pros: Easier, less pressure, can perfect each part
- Cons: Need to stitch together (but easy in iMovie)

**Option C: Multiple Full Takes**
- Record 2-3 complete takes
- Pick the best one
- Pros: Safety net, choose best performance
- Cons: Time consuming (30-45 min)

**MY RECOMMENDATION**: **Option B** (Scene-by-Scene)
1. Record Scene 1, stop
2. Review, re-record if needed
3. Move to Scene 2
4. Repeat for all 8 scenes
5. Stitch together in iMovie later (10 minutes)

### During Recording

**Voice Tips:**
- Speak **slightly slower** than normal conversation
- **Pause** between sentences (can trim silence later)
- **Smile** while talking (sounds friendlier)
- **Emphasize** key words: "Edge Impulse", "on-device", "real-time"
- **Project** your voice (not loud, just clear)

**Common Mistakes to Avoid:**
- âŒ Speaking too fast (demo feels rushed)
- âŒ Dead air for too long (seems unprepared)
- âŒ Umms and ahhs (re-record the scene)
- âŒ Clicking/breathing into mic (stay 6-8 inches away)
- âŒ Background noise (laptop fan, traffic)

**If You Make a Mistake:**
- **Small mistake (minor stumble)**: Keep going! Can edit later
- **Big mistake (wrong word, long pause)**: Stop, take a breath, restart that sentence
- **Scene doesn't work**: Stop recording, collect yourself, start scene over

- Change quality setting to "Maximum" in QuickTime dropdown
- Make sure iPhone is in focus (tap screen in QuickTime window)

**iPhone screen goes black during recording?**
- Settings â†’ Display â†’ Auto-Lock â†’ Never (temporarily)
